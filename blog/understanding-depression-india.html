Understanding Depression: What Every Indian Family Needs to Know

Netal Marothiya, Psychologist August 30, 2024 9 min read Bangalore

Depression is one of the most misunderstood conditions in India. Despite affecting an estimated 56 million Indians, it is still widely dismissed as laziness, weakness, or a phase that will pass. In my work as a psychologist in Bangalore, I see the damage this misunderstanding causes — both to the person suffering and to the families trying to help.

This article is for families, partners, and friends who want to understand what depression really is — and how to actually help.

What Depression Is (and Isn't)

Depression is a medical condition — a disorder of the brain and nervous system characterized by persistent low mood, loss of interest, and a range of physical and cognitive symptoms. It is not:

  • A character flaw or weakness
  • Being "overly sensitive"
  • Something that can be resolved by "thinking positively"
  • A result of not being grateful enough
  • Attention-seeking behaviour

Depression has biological, psychological, and social causes. It is influenced by genetics, brain chemistry, life events, trauma, and environment. And crucially — it is highly treatable.

Common Myths vs. Facts (Indian Context)

❌ Myth

"Just pray more / be more grateful. Depression is a spiritual problem."

✅ Fact

Faith can be a source of strength, but depression is a medical condition that requires professional treatment, just like diabetes or hypertension.

❌ Myth

"You have a good life — what do you have to be depressed about?"

✅ Fact

Depression is not caused by having a bad life. It can affect anyone regardless of income, success, or family situation. Brain chemistry doesn't check your privilege.

❌ Myth

"Seeing a psychologist means you're mad (pagal)."

✅ Fact

Therapy is a tool for anyone who wants to improve their mental wellbeing — just like going to a gym is for anyone who wants to improve physical health.

❌ Myth

"It will pass on its own. Just keep busy."

✅ Fact

Without treatment, depression can persist for months or years and worsen over time. Early intervention leads to significantly faster recovery.

Signs Your Loved One May Be Depressed

Depression looks different in different people. In Indian families, it is often masked by physical complaints or withdrawal. Watch for:

  • Persistent sadness, emptiness, or hopelessness lasting more than two weeks
  • Loss of interest in hobbies, food, relationships, or work they previously enjoyed
  • Fatigue and low energy even without physical exertion
  • Sleeping much more or much less than usual
  • Unexplained physical complaints — headaches, back pain, digestive issues
  • Difficulty concentrating, making decisions, or remembering things
  • Irritability, restlessness, or being easily frustrated (especially in men)
  • Withdrawing from family and social activities
  • Expressing feelings of worthlessness or excessive guilt
  • Talking about death, feeling like a burden, or not wanting to be here

How to Support a Family Member with Depression

Things That Help

✓ Listen without trying to fix

Sometimes the most healing thing you can do is simply say: "I hear you. I'm here." Resist the urge to immediately offer solutions or silver linings.

✓ Acknowledge their pain as real

Saying "your feelings are valid" and "I believe you" can be profoundly powerful for someone who has been told their depression is imaginary.

✓ Help with practical tasks gently

Depression makes basic tasks feel enormous. Offering to cook, accompany them to an appointment, or handle one small thing can reduce their load without pressure.

✓ Encourage professional help without ultimatums

Say: "I care about you and I think talking to a psychologist might help. Would you like me to help you find one?" — then let them decide.

Things to Avoid

✗ "Snap out of it" or "just be happy"

This communicates that their illness is a choice. It increases shame and makes it less likely they will open up to you again.

✗ Comparing their life to others

"So many people have it worse" invalidates their experience and deepens the guilt that's already part of depression.

✗ Withdrawing out of helplessness

It can be hard to know what to say. But silence and distance feel like abandonment to someone who is already struggling.

🆘 If someone is in crisis

If your loved one is expressing thoughts of suicide or self-harm, take it seriously and act immediately.

Treatment Works — and It's Available in Bangalore

The most important thing to understand is this: depression is one of the most treatable mental health conditions. With evidence-based therapy — particularly Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT) — the majority of people see significant improvement within 8–16 sessions.

If you or someone you love is showing signs of depression, please reach out. Early treatment makes a profound difference in recovery time and quality of life.
